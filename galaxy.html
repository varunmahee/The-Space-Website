Galaxies

A galaxy is a gravitationally bound system of stars, stellar remnants, interstellar gas, dust, and dark matter. The word is derived from the Greek galaxias literally "milky", a reference to the Milky Way. Galaxies range in size from dwarfs with just a few hundred million (10 to the power of 8) stars to giants with one hundred trillion (10 to the power of 14) stars,each orbiting its galaxy's center of mass.
